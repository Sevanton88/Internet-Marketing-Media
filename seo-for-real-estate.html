The real estate search funnel

How buyers and sellers search

Real estate has one of the longest search journeys of any industry. The same person might search dozens of times over months before contacting an agent. SEO is about being present at every stage.

Awareness stage

"Is it a good time to buy a house in [city]", "average home prices [area]"

Market report content and neighborhood guides capture these early-stage researchers and build brand awareness before they are ready to engage.

Consideration stage

"Homes for sale in [neighborhood]", "condos under $500k [city]"

Neighborhood and property type landing pages. This is where the majority of real estate SEO traffic volume sits.

Decision stage

"Best real estate agent [neighborhood]", "[Agent name] reviews"

Agent profiles, Google reviews, and local presence determine whether the lead converts to an actual client relationship.

Real estate SEO questions

How competitive is real estate SEO?

Highly competitive in major US markets, but with significant variation by neighborhood and property type. Ranking for 'homes for sale in [major city]' is extremely competitive. Ranking for specific neighborhoods, price ranges, or property types is often much more achievable and drives more qualified traffic anyway - buyers searching for 'condos under $600k in [specific neighborhood]' are much closer to a decision than those searching broadly.

Do individual agents need their own website or is a brokerage site enough?

Individual agents benefit significantly from their own website or at minimum a robust personal page on the brokerage site. Personal branding, specific neighborhood expertise, and agent-level reviews are difficult to express through a generic brokerage listing. The highest-performing agents in organic search almost always have a distinct online presence tied to specific areas.

How do you handle IDX integration and SEO?

IDX (Internet Data Exchange) listings present specific SEO challenges - MLS data is shared across many sites creating duplicate content, and dynamically-loaded listings are often not indexed by Google. We work around these constraints through proper canonical tags, supplementary neighborhood content, and ensuring that your unique value (market expertise, agent profiles, local guides) is what Google ranks.

How long does real estate SEO take to produce leads?

For neighborhood pages in less competitive markets, meaningful organic traffic can appear within 3-4 months. In major metro areas, 6-9 months is more realistic for competitive neighborhood terms. Agent profile and local SEO work (GBP, reviews, citations) often produces faster results - particularly for branded and agent-name searches.

Can you work with real estate teams or just individual agents?

Yes - both. Teams and brokerages add complexity (multiple agent profiles, multiple office locations, multiple target neighborhoods) but the fundamental strategy is the same. We scope the work based on how many agents, locations, and target areas are involved.

Is SEO worth it compared to Zillow and Realtor.com leads?

Portal leads (Zillow, Realtor.com) are expensive per lead and highly competitive - you are competing with every agent in the area for the same buyer. Organic SEO leads contact you directly, have higher intent, and cost nothing per lead once rankings are established. The best real estate businesses use portals for volume while building organic SEO as their long-term lead channel.
